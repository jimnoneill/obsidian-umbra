Read Heidegger's lecture on the cave ("[[Plato|Plato]]'s Doctrine of Truth") today. Strange essay.

- Heidegger thinks the cave marks the *beginning* of [[Metaphysics|metaphysics]] as the reduction of truth ([[Aletheia|aletheia]]) to correctness (orthotes). He says truth becomes judgment-truth, not disclosure.
- I find this overreading. [[Plato|Plato]] never defines truth as correctness in [[Republic|Republic]]. Heidegger is reading backward from late-modern problems.
- But: his point about the prisoner's *forced* ascent is real. The violence in the text deserves more attention than Anglo readers give it.
- Foucault's power/knowledge would have a field day with the cave guards.
