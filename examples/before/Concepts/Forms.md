# Theory of Forms

Plato's central metaphysical thesis. Forms (eide, ideai) are abstract, eternal, unchanging entities. Ordinary particulars are imperfect copies that 'participate' in Forms.

Examples: the Beautiful itself, the Just itself, the Triangle itself. Forms are the real objects of knowledge; particulars are the objects of opinion.

Problems: self-predication, the Third Man Argument, the problem of participation.
