# Socratic Method

Elenchus — refutation by cross-examination. [[Socrates|Socrates]] does not offer positive doctrines; he shows his interlocutors that they don't know what they thought they knew.

In later dialogues ([[Republic|Republic]], [[Phaedo|Phaedo]]) [[Socrates|Socrates]] becomes a mouthpiece for [[Plato|Plato]]'s own views. The method shifts.
