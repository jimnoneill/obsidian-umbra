# Allegory of the Cave

[[Republic|Republic]] Book VII, 514a-520a. The most famous passage in [[Plato|Plato]].

Prisoners are chained in a cave, facing a wall. Behind them, puppeteers cast shadows of objects on the wall, which the prisoners mistake for reality. A prisoner is freed, ascends out of the cave, sees the sun.

Interpretations: epistemological ascent, political critique of opinion, Platonic education, ontological hierarchy, therapeutic reorientation.
