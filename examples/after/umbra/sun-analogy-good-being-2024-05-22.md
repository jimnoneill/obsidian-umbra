# Sun Analogy and the Nature of the Good

> Source: [[05-22-2024]] · 2024-05-22

- Re-read the sun analogy ([[Republic|Republic]] 507a-509c). Three things: (1) the sun is cause of both being and becoming visible, (2) the Good is cause of both being and becoming knowable, (3) the Good is BEYOND being.
- That last part — epekeina tes ousias — is the most disputed phrase in [[Plato|Plato]]. Some scholars (Rosen) treat it as mystical, others (Schofield) read it deflationarily.
- My [[Thesis|thesis]] needs to take a position. I'll go moderate: the Good is beyond *being* in the sense of being a Form among [[Forms|Forms]], but it isn't mystical in the Plotinian sense.
