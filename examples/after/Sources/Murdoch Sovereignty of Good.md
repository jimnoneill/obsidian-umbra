# Murdoch — The Sovereignty of Good

Iris Murdoch's 1970 book. Three essays. Recasts [[Plato|Plato]]'s Good as an object of moral *attention*, not a premise in moral argument.

Key quote: 'The task of attention goes on all the time and at apparently empty and everyday moments we are re-fastening our vision.'

Heavily cited in my conclusion chapter.
