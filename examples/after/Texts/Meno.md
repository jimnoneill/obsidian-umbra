# Meno

Short dialogue. Contains the paradox of inquiry: how can you look for what you don't know?

[[Plato|Plato]]'s answer is recollection — illustrated by the slave-boy passage where [[Socrates|Socrates]] gets an uneducated boy to 'remember' geometric truths.

The cave offers a different solution to the same paradox: direct experience of the Good provides the starting point.
